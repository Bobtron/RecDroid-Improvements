csipsimple - issue #1926

Simultaneous Incoming Calls Ruins Ring and Voice

Posted on Aug 30, 2012 by Swift Monkey

What steps will reproduce the problem?

1.Start multiple calls to Csipsimple, either to multiple registered accounts in Csipsimple, or to one account that supports multiple concurrent calls. e.g. Google Voice forwarded to multiple numbers that are registered in Csipsimple. 2. If "Support multiples calls" is selected in the call options, the first arrived call will ring normal, and subsequent incoming calls will attenuate the ring to very low volume, or may switch it back to normal if there are more calls coming in. When one call is answered and other calls are dropped, the caller at the other end may hear your voice very low and distorted, depending on the codecs chosen and Media settings "use WebRTC implementation", "Audio implementation--Java, OpenSL", etc. 3. If "Support multiples calls" is not selected, the first arrived call will ring normal, and subsequent incoming calls will silence the ring. I haven't tried what will happen to the voice. 4. When multiple calls come in, Csipsimple tries to bring up multiple UI's (and tears down later for unanswered ones) for all the calls, which is very confusing and makes lower end phones sluggish or unresponsive for quite a while.

What is the expected output?

Only process the one call that arrives first (ring, UI), send "486 busy" to all subsequent incoming calls immediately without ring, UI, notification bubbles, etc. If the rejected incoming calls have the same caller ID number as that of the answered one (e.g. same last 10 digits because some caller ID have prefix "+", some have "+1", some have "1", some don't), don't even show a missed call notification. Anyway, when multiples calls come in, the UI and screen do not have enough space to show all of them big and easy to pick, and the user does not have enough time to discern and pick one. It is good enough to just serve the first one.

If a call comes in during another ongoing call, I prefer to flash a notification or a button somewhere on the current call's UI. A new incoming call UI blocks your operation on your current call's UI and you have no choice, either accept the new call or reject it right away.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system?

Thrill 4G 2.3.5. Mytouch 3G Cyanogenmod 6.
