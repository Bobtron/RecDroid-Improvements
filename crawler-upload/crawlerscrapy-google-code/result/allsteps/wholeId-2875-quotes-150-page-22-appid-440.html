csipsimple - issue #1947

Stabilize dialpad feedback

Posted on Sep 8, 2012 by Quick Giraffe

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. Dial any number to make a call 2. Some DTMF tones may be missed and the last one may continue to play 3. Press "Call" button, talk, end the call. The old DTMF tone may start playing again.

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? If DTMF tone is started, it must always end. Instead, some DTMF randomly may continue until you start another DTMF.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? CSS latest r1881. Android v2.2, HTC Aria

Please provide any additional information below.

In some occasions DTMF tone (when I dial a phone number to call) may be missed (omitted) at all (it's not a bid deal), and some may start and continue without the end. For example, I've seen many times occasions, when I dial a number and the last digit makes DTMF, which doesn't end. Then, I make the call, end it, and that DTMF just resumes its sound. If I click on "Home" button and go to phones home page (exiting CSS), I still hear DTMF. It will continue forever or until I re-open CSS again and press any digit to make another tone. Then it may stop and I may go back to home page.

It happens random, but quite often to notice. And of course, if it happens, you must do something to stop it...

If DTMF starts, there must be a guarantee that, it will be eventually stopped without user action. A class's destructor could be a good example of proper implementation here.

Comment #1

Posted on Sep 9, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Thanks for the point. Indeed the DialingFeedback class is a little bit messy. I should probably rewrite it, but for now I'll fix the bugs I can see reading the code : - The tones were not stopped before playing a new one (which may lead to miss some tones) - The timer task to stop the tone was done just after thread start instead after tone start (which may lead to never end the tone if timer task executes before tone) - On pause of the class (when ui is hidden) it's properly freed but not stopped. (which may lead to have infinite tone even after ui quit -- will not occur anymore thanks to fix of point 2, but better to stop anyway)
