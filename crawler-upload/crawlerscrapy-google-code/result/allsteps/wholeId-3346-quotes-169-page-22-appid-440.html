Comment #3

Posted on Jan 7, 2012 by Quick Hippo

It could be linked to the codec finally in use.

On incoming calls it's CSipSimple that has last world on codec to use. On outgoing the last words is given by the remote side. If the remote side choose a bad codec (not efficient), you'll get a bad audio quality. So my advise: First place an outgoing call. Press the little arrow below the call card, and choose the 'Information' button. I'll see a lot of information about the ongoing calls. And you should see the codec in use. Keep it in mind. Then end the call, and go in Settings > Media > Codecs

Here disable (long press on a row and choose disable) all codecs but the one that was used in your previous call.

Doing that, CSipSimple will announce to the remote side only the codec you left enabled. BTW, the remote side will be forced to choose the codec that has good quality. Because obviously csipsimple will not let the choose to the remote side ;).

This kind of case is frequent, and unfortunately, the way SIP works implies that depending on the way of the call, it's one or the other side that choose the codec. And if your remote side is not configured to select the codecs with same priority... nothing could be done on the client (csipsimple) side except announcing only one supported codec.
