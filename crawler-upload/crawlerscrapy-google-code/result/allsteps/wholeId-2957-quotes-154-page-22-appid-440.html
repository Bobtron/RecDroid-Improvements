csipsimple - issue #1858

FQDN is not supported in the SDP

Posted on Jul 31, 2012 by Helpful Giraffe

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. Receiving an INVITE or 200 OK with SDP content with a FQDN value in the SDP information. i.e. "c=IN IP4 caller.fooo.com" 2. Support of FQDN values in the Contact or the Via fields.

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? The application should issue a DNS query considering that FQDN are supported in the RFC3261. The application is unable to process the INVITE (does not signal an incoming call) or to answer with an ACK the incoming 200 OK with SDP.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? Hi,

Well, since I was kind of crafting my own SIP message I tested both scenarios. If I send an IP address in the SDP and the application works perfectly, on the other hand sending the C=IN IP4 domain.foo does not produce any result in the application. If the call was initiated by the other party, the INVITE that the app receives is not signaled and therefore no trying or ringing are forwarded. If the call is initiated by the app, the 200 OK with SDP that contains FQDN in the SDP, is not processed by the app and thus it doesn't generate the final ACK.

I hope this is valuable for you!
