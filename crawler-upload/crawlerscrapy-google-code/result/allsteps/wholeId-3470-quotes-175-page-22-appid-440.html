Comment #1

Posted on Nov 11, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Usually android OS doesn't give access to deep intergration. There is only the stock phone application (that hold the connection with the BT headset) that can capture BT buttons command. So third party apps can't get the info about the fact there is a BT button pressed. CSipSimple tries to registers to some event, but these events are not raised by all BT headset and on all android ROMs. I've several phone and several BT headsets. One of the headset has button working on some phones, and the other works on other phones. So that's not always very consistant, and depends deeply on the android ROM you're using. Unfortunately, if it does not work on your phone with your BT headset, I think that there is nothing that could be done by the app :S. I could try to workaround this kind of issue with devices connected to a pc to debug... But I fear that there's no miracle solution. Is there other voip apps on your phone that can get the BT headset button working?
