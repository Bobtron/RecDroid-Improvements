Comment #10

Posted on Jul 23, 2011 by Swift Camel

I found a forum posting on sipsorcery that explained a STUN server with TURN service: (numb.viagenie.ca) http://forum.sipsorcery.com/viewtopic.php?f=13&t=3280&hilit=numb.viagenie.ca

That took care of the audio issue, but the connection was unreliable, and at times CSS wouldn't register saying there was no response from the STUN server.

So what I did was route my SIP this way: GV > ipKall > SipSorcery > PBXes > CSipSimple on my Optimus V based on another posting from sipsorcery forums: http://forum.sipsorcery.com/viewtopic.php?f=6&t=2241&start=30

This actually solved the audio issue and was working great, but now for some reason I can't make outgoing calls all of a sudden...

so I'm back to doing other research.

Comment #11

Posted on Jul 30, 2011 by Grumpy Dog

On WIFI, with GV > ipKall > SipSorcery > PBXes > CSipSimple, I wasnt able to answer calls (PBXes was auto/UDP). I went to Expert setting and change PBXes to TCP. Then I was able to answer calls but it wasnt consistent. On the forum, I read that PBXes doesnot play nice. So I switched to Sipsorcery in TCP (ie GV > ipKall > SipSorcery > CSipSimple) I did some permutations: {with IPkall linked to ekiga stun = stun.counterpath.com turn = numb.viagenie.ca}

3G: irrespecitve of using wifi codec, -ICE ==> N audio out, Y audio in; +ICE ==> Y in, N out (ICE might be bec. of ekiga) WIFI: +ICE ==> N out, Y in; -ICE ==> Y in, Y out For now I am using TCP (Sipsorcery) + stun + turn -ICE

At some point, I will try replacing ekiga with another provider
