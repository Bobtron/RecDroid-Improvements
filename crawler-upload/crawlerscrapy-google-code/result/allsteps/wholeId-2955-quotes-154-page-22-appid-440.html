csipsimple - issue #1856

CSipSimple does not load when phone is powered on

Posted on Jul 30, 2012 by Happy Rabbit

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. Power on my LG P698 and wait until wifi is active. 2. CSipSimple does not load automatically (even after 10 minutes or more), and so my voip.ms account never sees a registration. 3. Toggle wifi off/on, then CSipSimple loads, registers with voip.ms and runs perfectly thereafter.

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? CSipSimple should load automatically when phone is powered on, after wifi is active (even though that takes a while), but it does not. I have configured CSipSimple to start based on wifi profile, and have also tried the always available option, but the phone-IP icon does not appear in upper left corner of the status bar and it never registers with voip.ms.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? CSipSimple version 0.03-01 r1108. OS: Android 2.3.4

Please provide any additional information below. My wifi is configured to never sleep. So, when I power on my phone, if I forget to cycle wifi off/on I will never receive any sip calls unless I leave the wifi signal area and then return, or unless I initiate a call myself.

Comment #1

Posted on Jul 30, 2012 by Happy Rabbit

I discovered what appears to be the cause of the problem. I had moved the app to the SD card to conserve precious storage space. When I move it back to the phone and power up, CSipSimple loads beautifully every time. Would someone else try this and see if you encounter the same issue? It would sure be nice to save the little space I have left in the phone's storage area for other apps.

Thanks!

Comment #2

Posted on Jul 30, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Indeed, storing to SDCard could produce various "weird" things.

It depends on the sdcard/device combination apparently. Sometimes it works flawlessly... but sometimes it makes the app working bad.

Google advise to not put service apps (that are intended to work always in background) on sdcard. But as users can use csipsimple just for outgoing calls (and that the app has options to run like that), it was decided to leave the user allowed to move the app to sdcard.

Issue 781 is about adding a warning when the app is installed on sdcard but the app configured to be available for incoming. So I guess that fixing the issue 781 will help to also solve the problem for your case since a warning would be presented in the interface to explain that it's risky to put the app on sdcard :).

Comment #4

Posted on Jul 31, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Also some interesting additional information, if you are looking for something lighter and know what is the CPU architecture of your phone (armeabi or armeabi-v7a), you can use a version of the app dedicated to the cpu architecture. If armeabi-v7a (most of recent phones) => http://nightlies.csipsimple.com/armeabi-v7a/ If armeabi => http://nightlies.csipsimple.com/armeabi/

There's about 2Mb of gain, it could be valuable ;).

The main version is "universal" so it contains two versions of the binary which is not very efficient ;)
