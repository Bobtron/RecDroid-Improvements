Comment #1

Posted on Apr 9, 2011 by Quick Hippo

That's not the choice I did. In fact, what you expect is just a question of point of view, and some users may expect the other behavior. Keep in mind that you configured the app to be available for outgoing calls.

Disconnect as you notice disconnect the app. If you launch again the app, or intent a call (if the app is configured for outgoing calls) it will restart the app.

What you really ask for is another feature : Having the app working only on a wifi BSSID. This kind of feature has been discussed here multiple times. I keep thinking that it should be performed by a plugin of the app. For example a plugin that integrate another "context aware" application (such as Locale or Tasker). By having this, we will have something much much more powerful than just a feature for only 5% of users inside the app, that will make it more complicated to configure. Unfortunately nobody took the point to implement that. I'll not implement something either for Tasker or Locale cause both apps are not OpenSource so implementing something will benefit one of these two software editor and I do the app to benefit users, not some company. However, if somebody wants to do it and does not have my ethic on what a software should provide to users he will be welcome, and I'll try to help to develop the plugin.

Note that it's not Quit, but it's Disconnect (unless there is something wrong in translation on your language ;) ). And it behaves exactly just like the Gtalk disconnect feature. It logout, but if you try to make a call (in gtalk im somebody), it restart the app.

Besides, there is another feature you may have not noticed : Long press on your home and add CSipSimple switcher widget. Select one of the accounts you have. It will add on the home screen a simple switcher to activate/deactivate this account. If there is no account anymore, the app will turn it off automatically. So virtually what you want can be performed quickly this way. Even if I really think it should be done through a plugin.

As for the disconnect option, it behaves as it should. I think that what your expect is linked to your configuration and you should not expect that but a feature that automatically change the csipsimple configuration to "only for outgoing" when you are outside your enterprise wifi BSSID.
