csipsimple - issue #692

Samsung Galaxy S Audio (Microphone) Issue - Verification Request

Posted on Feb 7, 2011 by Happy Rabbit

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1.Making and receiving calls

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? Normal conversation. Microphone acting out of the ordinary.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? Development version on Android 2.2

Please provide any additional information below.

I may have discovered what the problem might be with the microphone issue on Samsung Galaxy S series. When making a call, there are basically 4 different modes - 2 for the speaker and 2 for the microphone. When making a normal call, the phone is supposed to go into "non-speakerphone mode" where the incoming audio is sent to the earpiece and the microphone is on "short distance" mode. When on a speakerphone mode, the incoming audio is supposed to be sent to the outside speaker and the microphone is supposed to be set at "sensitive mode" where the user can talk at a distance.

When csipsimple is on "normal mode," the incoming audio is sent to the earpiece like it's supposed to do. However, the microphone is set at speakerphone mode where the outgoing audio is amplified to pick up voices from a distance. This obviously creates problems because now the outgoing audio is "overdeviating" and sounds are garbled. Every surrounding sound is picked up where the other party has difficulty making out what the caller is saying.

In order to solve this problem, csipsimple needs to properly set the microphone to "normal" mode when making or receving calls. It is not simply solved by using a third party audio management software. It is an inherent audio routing problem for the microphone which I believe could be solved by modifying the software.
