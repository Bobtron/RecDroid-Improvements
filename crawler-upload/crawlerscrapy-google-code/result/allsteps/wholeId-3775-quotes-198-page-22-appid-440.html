Comment #10

Posted on Aug 16, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Ok, my bad, your issue was not duplicate of 1014 actually.

In fact it is a well known behavior due to a limitation of your sip provider. Let me try to explain what happens.

Your sip provider allow to register multiple times to their service. But when a certain limit is reached, it just refuse new connection with a forbidden error.

Problem is that csipsimple, as a client that directly connect your service has to manage different IP. Say it connects first using a wifi hotspot. It will registers with some IP A. When wifi will come down on the android device, csipsimple will have no ways to de-register (since obviously no connection anymore). Then 3G comes up, csipsimple tries to register with IP B from the 3G connection... and here, your sip provider refuse to register cause they say there is too much registration for your account.

One solution would be to do something on the client to de-register all before registration. That's not really easy to manage with current sip stack, but that's something I have to look at. Another solution would be to ask your sip provider to manage this kind of situation on their side. For example, the user agent can be used to automatically de-register older registrations from csipsimple when they see another new registration instead of just saying "forbidden".

Comment #12

Posted on Aug 17, 2011 by Quick Hippo

revision 1005 should fix this problem (it will be built tonight).

It introduce a workaround for this kind of case, so requires configuration !

Change your account into an expert account (long press on account row, choose wizard, in generic section choose expert). Then edit account and select option "Try to clean registers".

This workaround will * do nothing if registers pass (when you have not yet reached limit of registered accounts allowed by your provider) * if register fails, it will unregister all existing registration on the server (csipsimple's ones but also ones from other sip clients if any), and then re-register.

It should workaround this case where old registers leaks. Obviously a cleaner way to solve this issue on csipsimple would be to store old registers when loose connection and then when connection is up again and that there is existing old registrations to remove these registrations. But this is not easy to do for now cause the underling sip stack does not allow that. So for now, it should do the trick, let me know how it goes.
