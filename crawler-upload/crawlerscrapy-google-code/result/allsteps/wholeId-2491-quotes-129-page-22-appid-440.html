Comment #1

Posted on Apr 7, 2013 by Quick Hippo

It's not a bug. The G729 codec is patented one. It was not clear for all users how the licensing works and put me (distributor of the app) in a not very clear situation where I distribute an app with g729 inside but tell users it's their responsibility if their activate or not the option and take a end user license for their own version of the app. As I don't want to get problems with how the app is distributed and that I found some kind company that was ready to produce and distribute a g729 plugin for csipsimple with the resell of the g729 codec patent inside, it was decided to drop from the mainstream the g729 codec support.

You can now at choice ; * download the g729 plugin from https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.csipsimple.plugins.codecs.g729 (note that the company is not me and I don't make any benefit on it, and from the price they resell the license I think they don't do any benefit either ;) ). * do your own build of the g729 codec plugin based on the source code and use it the way it's authorized for g729 when you build things by your own : just for tests/implementation purposes.

For more details about the price of the codec : http://www.sipro.com/Licensing-Terms-G-729.html (note that the "Royalty Based" has an initial fee of 15 000$)

Comment #3

Posted on Apr 21, 2013 by Quick Hippo

Yes g729 license is a real pain, that's why I discourage sip provider to use it. It handcuff users and people trying to provide software to users. The use of codec is something viral. When a sip provider push for a codec it pushes sip clients provider do deliver the codec ... and when it's a paid one, it force everybody to pay for something they didn't choose. That's because their model requires each endpoint have a license (so not only server side, even softphones). H owever, if you are a sip provider and already paid for a global g729 license (not linked to a specific software, you can do your own build of the csipsimple g729 codec plugin and install it on your phone). But it will only be valid only for you and your own usage.

About Silk is excellent codec ! It's the codec that comes from skype and is still used in skype. So it's very good and works well for low bandwidth. You should definitely try. It also use lower CPU than g729 (that is not optimized for ARM while silk is). It has great quality and low bandwidth usage; and it's an opensourced one that allows anyone to easily integrate and use it.
