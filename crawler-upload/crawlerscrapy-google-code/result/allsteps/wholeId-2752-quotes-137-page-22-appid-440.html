See http://code.google.com/p/csipsimple/issues/detail?id=1862#c1

To sum up position is to keep things inside csipsimple as rudimentary as possible and allow third party apps to do great apps to manage your record. Playing, transcoding, reorganizing files in different folders etc.

For now such app doesn't exists (AFAIK), but it's technically possible to do and having it as an opensource plugin of csipsimple would be great enhancement. But the idea is not to have it inside csipsimple and make it more complex. Idea of android is to have things modular ;).

Comment #3

Posted on Nov 29, 2012 by Quick Hippo

I think that I correctly understood.

My point is that it should not and will never be playable from inside csipsimple. It's not an application to manage sounds and do not pretend do as good as a dedicated app to play sound.

If it starts playing sound you'll start needing it to share files, to transcode files, to re-organize files etc. I definitely think that it will be 1000x better to have a dedicated app to play files recorded by csipsimple and allow to automatically transcode, reorganize upon users settings, archive and so on. Something dedicated that is thought to address this needs and cover these needs. Not reinventing the wheel inside csipsimple.

Again, android is made to be modular. You probably never noticed but for example contact app and phone app are actually 2 different distinct applications. (and the actual phone service is again another application). The OS is made so that apps can work together to bring great user experience. Trying to do monolithic apps that does 'everything' is in my opinion a big mistake often made by developers coming from iOS world. Besides having such approach with modular apps allows to bring more features to users and allows various ideas to be easily added without breaking the core app and without adding useless/complex features for users that do not use this feature.

Having such a different app would also allow to share dev effort with other sip/voip apps. And for example having one dedicated app to manage recorded logs from csipsimple, linphone, sipdroid, imsdroid (when they will implement this feature). BTW, could also work for apps that hack to record gsm calls.
