csipsimple - issue #551

Support for Narrow-Band Codecs

Posted on Dec 29, 2010 by Quick Ox

This is not an issue per se; it's more a question. I tried, but I could not find a forum or something similar for CSIPSimiple, so I am posting it here. If there is a more suitable venue, please point me to that direction.

I am not even sure about this, but CSIPSimple uses PJSIP and... PJMEDIA too, as part of it? If it does, does CSIPSimple have support for narrow-band codecs, particularly AMR? I do not see it listed in CSIPSimple, but is there a switch or something to turn it on? http://www.pjsip.org/sip_media_features.htm

I am trying to make VoIP work under a limited-bandwidth network. The advertised bandwidth is best-effort 300kbps. When the effective speed is close to that number, it works, but usually it is much, much lower. The upsteam is particularly low; it's very often below 100kbps.

I've tried Speex and iLBC but those did not work quite well. I wanted to try G729, but it is not supported on the server side. I am also looking forward to the SILK support you've started working on. If you (or any of the readers) have recommendations, I'd love to listen.

Thanks!

Yasuro

Comment #1

Posted on Dec 29, 2010 by Quick Hippo

For all "IPP codecs", it's technically impossible to get it working as it on android cause it's "Intel" codecs and does not work on ARM architecture.

So we have to do the portage for all of these codecs but it take a lot of time. I and siphon author (the equivalent project on iPhone) do portage of most asked codecs. G729 was done by the author of siphon and I ported it to android but that's not the one provided by pjsip in pjmedia.

It goes the same way for AMR and others.
