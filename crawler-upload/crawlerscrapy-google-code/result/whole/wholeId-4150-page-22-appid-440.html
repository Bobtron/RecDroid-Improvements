well, if your sip provider support it, if they are always media gateway for all calls and if you do not receive direct sip calls you can activate g729 only. Posted on Mar 31, 2011 by Happy Giraffe

I'm not sure if you're right both in that question... Because, servers admin normally make disallow=all in all over sip configuration, then restart order. That mean, whatever order you give in your client, your server admin will reset your order to re-order codecs as he established and according to his server preferences.

In that issue the are tow tasks, first, normaly servers admin disallow all codecs, then re-order them giving g729 first category, or after H264 or else if they support video call. So with this manner is your service provider who reoder your codecs negotiation, sure, according your activated codec.

I mean, what you have to do is only activate all codecs possible, whatever the order is, and your server will take the task to negotiate who's first, whatever you give first order...

Regarding the license issue, you have to verify if your service provide that service codec, and have that license, because it doesn't make sense that you purchase a license to use it with a specific provider who don't provide that codec, so it's recommend it that you contact your provider to verify before if they support such as codec, and if they have multichannel, so it's enough that you just activate it, because the server license it's enough to cover you as a client user. regards,
