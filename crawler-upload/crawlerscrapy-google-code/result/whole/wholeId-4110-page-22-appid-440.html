csipsimple - issue #708

Not working when HTC Legend is connected to WiFi

Posted on Feb 11, 2011 by Helpful Camel

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. Connect HTC Legend mobile phone to WiFi

What is the expected output? What do you see instead?

Expected outcome it that calls can be made and received. Instead, incoming calls are not received. For outgoing calls, when a number is dialed, the HTC shows the "calling" screen, but never progresses to "ringing", and after a period hangs up. The phone being called does not ring.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? 0.01-01 r615 on Android

Please provide any additional information below. - Works ok when phone is not connected to wireless, whether or not "Use WiFi" is enabled in the Network settings. - If the phone is connected to WiFi, and "Use WiFi" settings are enabled, VOIP account is shown as registered, but calls cannot be made or received as described above. - If the phone is connected to WiFi, and "Use WiFi" settings are disabled, the VOIP account is shown as "inactive" and cannot register.

Comment #3

Posted on Feb 13, 2011 by Quick Hippo

On the public access library, I think that almost all port are blocked excepted 80 (for http), so none sip app may be able to register.

On you wifi access point, as you probably configure it, there is things to test.

First you could test to change RTP port in CSipSimple configuration. * Go in settings. Press menu, "Expert Mode" (it will turn on expert mode for settings ;) * Then go in Network section, and scroll to RTP port. By default it is set to 4000. You could try to change it to 50000 for example (something much higher).

However, as you said sometimes SIP does not register I fear that there something more blocking on your firewall that prevent more than just the RTP port. It could be something that is expected to prevent DoS (denial of service) attacks but that also block UDP VoIP. There is also some router with know issues regarding their SIP support. For example the WRT54G with a firmware < v6. So, if you have the complete control of your router access point : First thing to do is to check the latest version of its firmware. Then you could try to disable temporarily the firewall (it should be done only for test, and don't forget to turn it on just after the test ! ). Also, as there is still NAT, use the STUN option (see the first entry of the FAQ wiki page to know how).

If firewall turned off help, you'll have to investigate why firewall on block you. Maybe port are forbidden, maybe types of packets are forbidden or maybe dos attack configuration prevent you from using SIP voip.
