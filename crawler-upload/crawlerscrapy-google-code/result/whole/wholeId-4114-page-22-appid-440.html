Comment #1

Posted on Feb 13, 2011 by Quick Hippo

First, I think that echo is a consequence of delay. In ExpertSettingMode (see wiki page) you can set echo tail length to something higher to increase the tail of audio treaten as possibly containing echo. Also if your device support floating point (I don't know if optimus one do), you could test speex echo mode. Also reducing sound amplification on your side (or not using rear speaker if used) may help to reduce echo on the other side.

As for delay now, the most probable cause of this is network latency. Unfortunately, that's linked to the fact VoIP use IP... If the IP network is slow you'll observe delay on VoIP applications. It could also be linked to the audio driver of the device that does not support low latency, but if you experiment sometimes good and sometimes bad results, it's more likely due to network quality.
