Posted on Mar 3, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Yes forcing PCM was a very good idea. It often help to get rid of SDP negociation :)

What I think that happens (has to be confirmed, but I had another bug report about a GPX and was the reason) : Greastream are known to be buggy regarding UPDATE method that they actually announce to support (that's a real bug they have and it's not SIP compliant).

On it's side, pjsip (the sip stack csipsimple use, but that is used in many other sip client on pc,mac,iphone etc), does not allow to communicate with several codecs at a time while Grandstream actually has this capacity.

So during codec negociation if the call is initiated from csipsimple it will propose all its codecs. The grandstream that does support communicating with several codec at a time will reply with a list of codec. That's still good at this point, grandstream has perfectly right to do that and to not choose the codec yet. However, since pjsip does not supports that (yet), it will send an UPDATE (or RE-INVITE depending on features of remote part), to fix only one codec. At this point it should also be perfectly good, one sip client has right to choose to change or select one codec at any time during a call (here it's at begginging).

The problem is that grandstream at this point will ignore this update. And that's a bug on their side. Then CSipSimple (pjsip) thinks that it's good and that it can communicate with the seleced codec, however the grandstream will send media encoded with another codec and pjsip will not be able to decode.

I found a workaround to that (at least for the Grandstream device that had this problem). It's available in nightly builds (http://nightlies.csipsimple.com/trunk/) and you have to ensure in ExpertSettingMode (global settings) that the option Network > Force no reinvite is activated.

Well, that's just my first analysis and without logs I can't confirm it's the problem. But I already had this kind of situation and the fact that it works forcing PCM codec make me thinking that it could be right.

If you can collect logs (see HowToCollectLogs wiki page) I'll be able to see exactly what happens and tell you if it's really the problem, and if not there is maybe another workaround better than forcing codec possible :)
