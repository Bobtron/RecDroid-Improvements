Comment #6

Posted on Dec 28, 2010 by Quick Hippo

Just something that may help you to understand some behaviors : RTP and SIP INFO does not produce sound. It's informations sent by another way. In band generation simulate the sound and send it as if it was produced by micro.

I guess that your ATA phone behaves like my "Auto" mode in RTP mode. (It tries RTP and if not acknowledge by other side fallback to in-band generation). However some sip gateways transform RTP or INFO into sound if the sound then need to be sent to another network. So usually the more reliable way to send is to use in-band even if it's less robust to low bandwidth and sound distortions due to the network. (Reason why by default it's Auto = RTP and if fail in-band -- however some gateway announce to support RTP but doesn't actually transform RTP to in-band on their side, and as far as I understood from other users that have a Gizmo account, it's the case of gizmo : they announce to support RTP but actually does not => in this case you have to choose "In band generation which will enforce inband)

One thing strange in your descriptions is that sometimes you hear the tones produced by csipsimple on csipsimple side which should never happen (unless there is something wrong on my code). I'll check that and keep you in touch.

Another possibility if tones are messy on the other side is that codec transform it badly. What codec are you using? (some codecs are optimized for speech and may truncate high frequency dtmf tones). (Press the little (i) button while in call to know what codec is currently in use).
