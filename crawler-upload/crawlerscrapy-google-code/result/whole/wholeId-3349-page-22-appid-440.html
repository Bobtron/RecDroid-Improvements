csipsimple - issue #1449

Wishlist stuff

Posted on Dec 21, 2011 by Swift Hippo

I have three suggested features for CSipSimple:

First, thinking of the people who will never understand what a dialing plan is but still want to have the dialer finish their numbers for them and place calls with the best or cheapest carrier:

1) when someone dials a number or partial SIP address, check both the address book and call logs for matches. If there's only one match, connect it, otherwise show the same matches drop given by the textmode dialer. Currently the CSipSimple dialer matches numbers from the wrong end. For example 1250123457 in the phone book, typing the local part 1234567 doesn't match, but 1250 does. AFAIK there's currently no SIP URL matching at all.

2) for each outbound call, record the user's choice of provider. When they choose the same one twice in a row, make it the default for that number/URL. To call via another provider they can manually select the provider immediately before dialing (implies that the dialer remembers whether the provider was chosen manually or is just showing the first SIP account.)

Between those two, even my 78-year-old mother could get most of the benefits of dialing plans without ever touching the filters.

Lastly, a wishlist item for the folks who don't have native SIP support in their phone, for example everyone with Froyo or earlier, where it's impossible to enter a SIP URL into the contact list:

3) If the CSipSimple dialer searched the contacts for email addresses matching (<sip:[^>*]>) they could enter SIP urls as email addresses to work around their old version of Android. Failing this, having all the options in item 1 above would at least give them matches out of their call logs, which would save a lot of keymap swapping to type awkward SIP URLS for carriers with non-numerical usernames like Ekiga and others who even charge extra to have non-numerical IDs, lol.
