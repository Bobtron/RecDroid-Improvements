csipsimple - issue #1140

Often do not get "Ringing" sound

Posted on Jul 11, 2011 by Happy Giraffe

I have had good success with CSipSimple in Australia, but I noticed that sometimes there was no "Ringing" sound as I waited for the call to connect via PennyTel

I am currently in Japan and almost all calls do not "Ring" while my HTC Wildwire is dialing out. My Laptop based software does not have this problem, so I guess it is Android OR CsipSimple related. The normal Android phone software does not exhibit this issue.

People I call suggest that their phone rings, but when they answer it sounds as if nobody is at at my calling end. Sometimes the very first thing I hear is the person at the other end speaking. It is a pain being "charged" for calls that don't work.....

Any suggested remedies or similar experiences??????

Comment #2

Posted on Jul 16, 2011 by Quick Hippo

It's more likely about too big UDP packets.

CSipSimple, unlike the stock sip application supports a lot of codecs. As consequence it has to announce that to remote side (if all codecs are activated). As consequence it will try to send big UDP packets. Unfortunately some routers does not allow too big udp packets and just does not transmit the packet to the remote side. As consequence your call invite will never reach server and csipsimple will remain in a state waiting for ack from server.

To solve this issue several ways : * You can try to activate the "Use compact mode" option (in settings > network). It will reduce SIP INVITE packet size. If that's supported by your carrier it may reduce enough to have UDP packets going through network without problems. If it works with your sip provider maybe I could add a wizard for this provider with Use compact mode activated by default.

  • If your sip provider does not support compact sip mode. You should try to reduce number of enabled codecs. By default csipsimple comes with big capabilities in terms of codecs. However, most sip providers and remote parties does not support all of these codecs. For example SILK codec (the one of Skype), is not widely used yet (even if it provide good perfs). So you could disable useless codecs (the one you know your provider does not support), in Settings > media > codecs (long press on a codec row to disable it).

You should also use latest nightly version : http://nightlies.csipsimple.com/trunk/
