Comment #1

Posted on May 12, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Are you sure you are using the correct list? CSipSimple provide two codec lists : one for narrow band and one for wide band (by default mobile data is considered as narrow and wifi as wideband). Other apps does not have this feature so you may have missed that point.

If you are sure of you configuration, could you provide me some logs (see the HowToCollectLogs wiki page).

In fact there is usually two ways to manage codecs priority. Now (in recent versions), CSipSimple behaves to consider its own list as the one that should be taken into account with the higher priority. But previously it had a lower priority than the one of the remote side.

In fact to understand how codec priorities works you have to understand how sip SDP works.

When you invite somebody, you send him the list of the codec you support (with the order you prefer). The other side, choose in this list.

So depending how both client are configured results could vary. For example if you receive a call from somebody that tell you what are the codecs they prefer, you can either choose to follow their preferred codec or choose on your list of codecs the one you prefer and that is supported by the other side.

When you make a call, you can do less things. You can only send to the remote party what are your preferred codec and let them choose.

If you provide me logs, I'll be able to know exactly in which case you are, and if there is actually something that is going wrong in CSipSimple (actually in pjsip).

Comment #3

Posted on May 12, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Ok, that's a little bit more clear (even if in logs there is not the beggining of the INVITE so that I can see exactly what is the first reply of your server, but I guess what it is).

It's due to the fact pjsip does not manage multiple codecs offers. Your sip provider replies without choosing any codec. In this case, pjsip tries to force one codec (the prefered one - g722). For some reason your sip server refuse this and send only G711. (rtp packets are of type 8 (g711) instead of being 9 (g722).

Sounds like if the server is not RFC compliant regarding SDP negociation. The limitation on pjsip side exists (and should be fixed by pjsip guys), but even with this limitation, it behaves a RFC compliant way the sip server does not understand.

So on my side nothing can be done. It is either relevant of the pjsip project (the sip stack on which csipsimple relies for now - more sip stack support is planned in the future) or on your server side that does not behaves a consistent way. If you can produce me one more log (direct send using email instead of publishing on the issue list, it's better for your privacy and provide me more infos). I'd like to understand how other sip stack could work with this cause regarding logs I have here the remote side does not support g722 at all in the second sdp offer they send while refusing the force of the g722. Unless your provider do independent flows, and that others sip stack just fake you and make you think there is g722 both way, while there is g722 out and g711 in ;).

Comment #7

Posted on May 13, 2012 by Quick Hippo

yes logs could help but I have my idea on what could go wrong : UDP packet can be blocked by network equipement if packet size is too big. Announcing an extra codec increase INVITE packet size. If your 3G carrier is very strict it could drop invite packet and your call will timeout. To reduce packet size the "Compact mode" option (in expert setting mode) could help to reduce size, but it has to be supported by SIP provider/remote party else you'll observe worse things.
