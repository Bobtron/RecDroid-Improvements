Comment #1

Posted on Jun 30, 2012 by Quick Hippo

I didn't get what is the need here?

You list some non SIP providers. CSipSimple focuses on the open protocol SIP. This protocol supports voip, messaging, presence, video calls, and many other stuff. Not everything is yet implemented inside CSipSimple but it's on the road ;).

In the other apps you mention not all use SIP, are not all really open (in the way I consider it). They are most of the time linked to a service. CSipSimple is not linked to any Service nor any SIP provider. It leave users really free, and users that wants to communicate with them also free to choose a different provider. That's the aim of SIP. It's about users freedoms. They could even have their own SIP server if they want. Apps like Skype or Viber capture users, and requires other users that want to communicate with them to open an account using their app too.

About specific point of balance credit. That's tricky. The SIP RFC has only some mention on where it should be done, but doesn't yet normalize how. So, for now, in csipsimple there is some wizards that already support that because the sip providers give an API to get the balance credit. And it's implemented when it can be. As there is nothing normalized it means one implementation by provider. In ideal world I wish something will land in SIP protocol, so that CSipSimple can just support this part of sip protocol and then it will be up to SIP providers to conform this part of the specification if they want to provide to their users balance credit feature.
