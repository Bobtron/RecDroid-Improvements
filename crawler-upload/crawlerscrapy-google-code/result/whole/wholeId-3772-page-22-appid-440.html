csipsimple - issue #1061

Pressing the power button twice hangs up call (LG Optimus One)

Posted on Jun 15, 2011 by Quick Cat

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1.Make a phone call on LG Optimus One 2.Press the power button 3.Press the power button again

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? I expected the screen to turn on but did not expect the call to disconnect. First time you press the power button the screen turns off. Second time the screen turns on and the call is disconnected.

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? CSS 0.02-02 r913, Android 2.2.1 on LG Optimus One This doesn't seem to happen with Android 2.3.4 on Nexus One

Please provide any additional information below. I actually came across this while on hold for a long time. The screen had already turned off and I hit the power button to turn on the speaker phone. That worked, so I put down the phone and eventually the screen turned off again as it should. Then when someone finally answered I wanted to turn off the speakerphone so I hit the power button again to turn on the screen and although the screen turned on the call also disconnected. Ouch. Not exactly what I was expecting.

It seems to be every time you hit the power button twice, it hangs up the call.

Other than that csipsimple amazing and getting better all the time!

Comment #1

Posted on Jun 16, 2011 by Quick Hippo

I guess the power button is also the hangup button isn't it?

In fact a third party application (other than stock applications), can't register the press of the hangup button when the screen goes off (but only when it goes on).

As consequence android applications that want to handle the hangup button can only do that after the second press of the hangup button. Previously the hangup button was not managed by csipsimple and I added the implementation that is done in other sip clients to manage the hangup button on second press (see issue 605).

I'll try to remove the KEY_POWER event from the list of events that can hangup the call, but I fear that it will not be enough. The only other thing I could do then will be to add an option (cause some users actually want the hangup button of their phone to do something, even if they need to press twice).
