Comment #1

Posted on Jul 18, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Normally, if correctly detected by the sip stack, it should show the account name that match the incoming call.

Obvisouly since SIP is a peer to peer protocol, remote party can contact your sip client directly which made impossible to detect which account match incoming call. However in most configurations it is possible and so you should be able to see the account name just below the remote party name in the contact call card. So if you choose one account display name "business" and the other "personal" you will see the corresponding name in the incoming call.

Comment #3

Posted on Jul 19, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Just quick point about 3CX, it also use pjsip sip stack (but 3CX is closed source unlike csipsimple). So for this kind of issue, what you observe on 3CX is what you'll observe on CSipSimple (both rely on the same sip stack).

About debugging the issue, you can try to collect logs when receiving calls (see HowToCollectLogs wiki page), and send it (mentionning this issue number in the mail ;) ). I'll have a look to sip traces and tell you what could be the root of what you observe.
