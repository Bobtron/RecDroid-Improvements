Comment #3

Posted on Nov 8, 2013 by Quick Hippo

Previous android versions did allow to hook the internet call. Unfortunately they removed the permission for third party apps (non system app) to hook 'internet call' field action (at least in stock android contact app). That's why it's now advised to use the IM field instead of the 'internet call' field.

Comment #5

@rtrelea:

In my trials of using either the native dialer or the Contacts/People address book on the phone, tapping an Internet Calling address for a contact will prompt to complete the call with either the built in android Internet Calling, or CsipSimple. It works as you were asking for it to to work. My phone offers the choice of making it permanent "always" or "just once".

Even if CSipSimple is not running at the time, if you select it, CsipSimple will launch, connect, and make the outgoing call, and when the call is over, it will terminate. (If it was already running, it just goes back to waiting for a call).

So, unless I misunderstand what you are asking, it already works that way.

(My tests on Android 4.2.2).
