Comment #4

Posted on May 31, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Yes in this case, incoming call are not impacted. It's due to how sip works :

When you make an outgoing call, your side (csipsimple) will announce to remote side the codecs it supports. In case of csipsimple it can be big because many codecs are supported. I try now to activate as less as possible, and leave then users compare with codecs supported by their provider to select only the best regarding their needs. But was not the case previously : I naively allowed all supported codecs.

On the other side, when you have an incoming call, it's the other side that propose possible codecs. Usually there is only a little list of codecs, because sip providers often only send "best" codecs for their needs and limited by their server capabilities. Then, CSipSimple will pick in this list only one codec, and reply with only this codec. So this packet will be very small.

That's why it's not a symmetric behavior. Besides it depends a lot on router in the middle, and mobile carrier are now known to set this value to a small one to reduce their network load. That's why now, when I see a problem with only outgoing on mobile network or limited network, I often think about this problem.

There is several ways to fix it. One is to reduce codecs list announced by CSipSimple. CSipSimple also support a "Compact header" + "Compact SDP" feature (known as "Compact SIP" in network settings), that also significantly decrease packet size. But it has to be supported by SIP provider. That's why it's not activated by default. Another solution is to switch to TCP transport (which has almost no limit on packet size), but, also, it has to be properly supported by SIP provider.

I understood all of that after a lot of tests, and didn't update this issue just after understanding that. So my apologizes for the big delay to treat the issue. And thanks for reporting the issue with other issues on the subject it helped me to ring a bell on how to reproduce this kind of problem.
