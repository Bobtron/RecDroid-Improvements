Comment #1

Posted on Apr 29, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Actually there is always on "local" account to allow some provider that try direct calls (and does not use the register socket to send the invite). As consequence the local account remains active til next restart of the app when a complete check of numbers of active accounts is done.

However, you're right, it should work as you expect, but for now, that's not really a bug but due to the fact the feature is designed like that now. Modifying this could introduce hudge regressions so I'll not try to fix that before the next market update (that will come soon).

Other point, for market release I'll introduce a feature to activate / deactivate the feature "multiple calls". So that your first problem will disappear.

In fact, when you register your sip provider it remembers your IP in order to be able to reach you again later. If for some reason you loose connection or disconnect fails, csipsimple has no way to send an unregister to your sip server, and when you re-register you are in a state where your provider has two entries for your device.

In an ideal world your provider should check multiple registrations and ensure it does not comes from the same IP+user agent and just drop the oldest registration if so.

But well, if you are in this case, your sip provider will recieve one call, and send to all registred entries (the old csipsimple one, that never get unregistered), and the new one. So, as I said, the option to allow only one call at a time will virtually solve the first problem you observe.
