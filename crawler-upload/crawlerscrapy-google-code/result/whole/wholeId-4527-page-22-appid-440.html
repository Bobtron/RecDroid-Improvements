Comment #10

Posted on Nov 14, 2010 by Quick Hippo

Re-merged with issue 136 : just to centralize things (this is a part of global tls/ssl effort).

@zatya : tcp actually works, but I think that you are trying to use the option available in settings, while that's not the good way to use TCP. TCP is a settings that has to be activated in account settings. In global settings you just choose which transport you want to activate for the software, but actually doesn't say that an account should use TCP. To do so, transform your account into a advanced account, and activate TCP. You should (must) keep UDP & TCP transport both activated in settings (UDP is still used for DNS SRV & STUN). Your sip provider (registrar) must support tcp ... if not, will not work of course...
