csipsimple - issue #323

app changes Via and Contact to UK Ministry of Defense IP during authentication

Posted on Oct 29, 2010 by Swift Bird

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. install from android market 2. i've only got it in one packet capture. i can't seem to reproduce on new or existing accounts. 3. i have yet to go through the source available from svn 4. i have not tried an uninstall and re-install 5. i have forcefully killed the app to see if it was a on-start type of thing. 6. i did not have logcat going when i got this packet capture

What is the expected output? What do you see instead? i expect my account information should not be sent to a 3rd party. the contact and via headers should not be manipulated like this. this could be an attempt at stealing mass amounts of sip credentials. did this come from this project or an upstream lib?

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system? 0.00-15 cm 6.1 rc1

Please provide any additional information below.

here's the packet capture taken directly on the phone with my public ips changed to 200.200.x.x and 100.100.x.x. the 192.168.1.31 is the phone and the 25.128.26.56 is the one that was injected into the initial registration then removes itself after successful auth. luckily that network is not being announced right now but I'm checking to see any recent hijacking attempts.

I'll have a closer look to your traces and tell you if I find something. On your side check the source code but I guess you'll find nothing. At least I did nothing to do such a privacy leak and pjsip code is widely use by other project.

Just a question, are you living in UK or is your sip provider (registrar) in UK?

Just fyi I'm living in France and never observed such a contact rewritting. But really sounds to be linked to a network equipment or something with your registrar (or it's maybe simply a public IP of a router between you and the registrar.... a traceroute could help you in this case to see that).
