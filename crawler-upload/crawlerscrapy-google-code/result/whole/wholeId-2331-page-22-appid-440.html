Comment #4

Posted on Aug 29, 2013 by Quick Hippo

From the picture NetGSM integrates the "crappy" way.

The way they integrated (called the "privileged intent" way) prevent other apps to integrates.

Google emailed all developers of application integrating this way and asked them to update their app with that removed. They also posted a blog post about that : http://android-developers.blogspot.fr/2013/05/handling-phone-call-requests-right-way.html

I also discussed with guys at google and the way CSipSimple behaves is the good one. The apps like netgsm that integrates using the priviledged intent should be disabled for regular case. (The biggest fear of Google about these app is that they could block Emergency calls). There is however valid case where the privileged intent way should be use (basically when the device has no GSM layer -- and that's what I raised to google guys when they started to focus on this topic before they removed all apps with this capability).

So... In short what you could do :

Solution 1 : Disable the crappy way netgsm integrates (hopefully if they didn't made things a too wrong way, there is a setting somewhere to disable their approach).

Solution 2 : Well, in the screenshot you have, you can choose "Always" and pick the "Phone" Option. (You'll see then another popup that is one of csipsimple).

Solution 3 : make csipsimple behaving the same crappy way than NetGSM.. it's what you've seen in the warning tab... and that's why there is a warning tab in the app : this warning tab only appear because csipsimple detects there is an app behaving the wrong way... If you read the indications of this warning tab you should have a condensed version of what I'm currently explaining. If you enable the "non clever way" option (that is also available in settings > intergration when ExpertSettingMode is enabled), you'll get csipsimple shown at the same first popup than NetGSM. This last solution is highly discouraged because you'll also lost the cool feature available in Filtering/rewriting rules that is pretty cool to avoid popups each time you dial and get the app clever when dialing. It will also make csipsimple behaving a wrong way in its integration to android system.
