Comment #1

Posted on Dec 19, 2010 by Quick Hippo

Well, actually it's not "standard android dialpad" just the same look ;).

There is several ways in SIP to send tones. CSipSimple try to choose the best way and sometimes (2 method out of the 3 possible method) doesn't send any real "audio" tone. (can be sent through RTP or SIP INFO -- you can choose in settings DTMF mode). However it's absolutely useless to play the tone or your side. Besides due to possible latency of sip calls, it would be extremely risky to play tone on your side :

As it could be considered by remote party as another tone played, it could lead to a situation when the remote party consider that there is 2 tones ! So really to risky to have such an option.

Besides, digits dialed should be shown on screen... so hearing the tone is not really needed ... Could be convenient but as it would be risky, I'd say that's a bad idea.

If I get time I'll add an option for that but would really advise people to not use it.
