Comment #1

Posted on Jan 12, 2012 by Quick Hippo

Usually this kind of problem is due to the fact the sip provider needs a special way to enter international prefix.

Many sip client automatically add a rewriting rule hard coded that transport + in 00. Maybe their pc software automatically do that.

If so, don't worry, csipsimple can also automatically rewrite numbers when call is made from stock dialer or a contact number. You can have a look to Settings > filtering rules.

But before doing, that you can validate with the csipsimple dialer and try to make the call with 00 instead of + (or ask the sip provide what is the prefix supported by their sip platform). Then simply add your rule in the configuration :).

But normally it is not something with csipsimple. If it works for land area, no reason to not work for international unless the number dialed is not understood by the sip provider.
