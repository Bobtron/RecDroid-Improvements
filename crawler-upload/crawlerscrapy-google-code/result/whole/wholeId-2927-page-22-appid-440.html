Thanks for the issue. It's a very important point. I often try to evangelize users I know so that they take a very big care on what applications requires as permission. I think that something in google play is missing to allow developers to explain as it's done in the wiki page because it's not always obvious. I also often advise them to use opensource apps that can be reviewed by users-developpers and that usually have no commercial/service goal.

Sometimes there is even involuntary permission leaks (even in very big apps such as Skype). On proprietary apps it's often used to go against users by developers that tries to hack. On opensource apps, it should be found and reported to be fixed more easily by developers that doesn't aim to hack it first but rather to improve it. That's why firefox and chrome are and will always be better in security than internet explorer for example ;)
