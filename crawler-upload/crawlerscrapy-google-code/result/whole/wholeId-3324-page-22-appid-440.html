Samsung Galaxy S, choppy sound on receiving end

Posted on Jan 9, 2012 by Happy Wombat

What steps will reproduce the problem? 1. make an outgoing call 2. 3.

What is the expected output? What do you see instead?

Expected is that the person receiving my call has the same audioquality as I do (i.e. perfect sound). Instead the receiver always has a choppy sound, right from the beginning. When I receive a call, we both have good sound!

What version of the product are you using? On what operating system?

CSipSimple: 0.03-01 r1108 (latest from Android Market) Android: Gingerbread v2.3.3 Samsung Galaxy S GT-i9000

Please provide any additional information below.

Working with PCMA and G729, over Wifi or G3, always the result is the same: choppy sound at the receiverÂ´s end when I initiate the call. When the call is initiated at the other side, there is no problem at all. I have played around with all possible settings I think: clock rate of 32 Khz gives the best sound. Keep awake on or off makes no difference, I use for micro sound the IN_COMMUNICATION setting but I have tried the other settings as well. Since the problem is only there when I initiate the call, I have the feeling it might have something to do with the network settings. Packets that get lost regularly?

Any help or suggestion would be highly appreciated! BTW - I really LOVE this app and how it is integrated in Android!

Update to my earlier post: after a long conversation with the helpdesk of the SIP provider, Xs4all, it looked like changing some network settings in CSipSimple did improve the sound quality on both ends of the conversation.

However, here is what actually is the situation now: 1. calls with a subscriber on the same SIP server, sip.xs4all.nl, indeed are perfect. 2. calls to subscribers outside this particular SIP server still suffer from this chopped sound, and occasionally ticking noise. With these ongoing problems making VoIP cals, I also subscribed to VoipBuster, and with that service there is no problem at all. Perfect sound on both sides of the conversation on my Galaxy S with CSipSimple, be it with some latency. What causes these problems when using sip.xs4all.nl is beyond me, I created a ticket for technical support and wait for their answer ...

So all in all I now do NOT believe that the issues I experienced were due to CSipSimple (or the Galaxy S) !

Comment #4

Posted on Jan 15, 2012 by Happy Wombat

Found the problem. It is in the use of the PCMA codec. Whether on Wifi or on 3G, that codec always gives that choppy and sometimes crackling sound when connecting to someone who is not also on sip.xs4all.nl. However, when connecting with G729, it always works on both sides of the connection without any audio problems.

I found out that in negotiating a connection with sip.xs4all.nl, that server ALWAYS by default connects to the highest possible codec that is enabled, whatever the order of the codecs is in CSipSimpleÂ´s wideband and narrowband settings. So I disabled all codecs except G729 and now indeed that codec is used, and all calls work fine.

Just to be complete for anybody else using Xs4all as provider: with only codec G729 enabled, the voicemail cannot be approached, not for listening to recorded messages in my own voicemail box, nor for recording new messages with another subscriber using that same sip.xs4all.nl. Both are only possible with PCMA enabled.
