Comment #1

Posted on Jul 6, 2011 by Quick Hippo

Hi Vijay,

Actually, MagicJack use a special registration way that is not published.

In previous versions of csipsimple I introduced a hack to use their registration method -- it was a hack in the standard registration process to do the registration the way it is expected by MagicJack servers. This method was found by some hackers on MagicJack community.

Unfortunately, recently MagicJack changed the way to do this registration. It sounds, they really don't want standard sip client to use their service. That's a shame... but we can't do anything against that. Until somebody find a new hack to perform registration on their server, there is nothing I can do. That's obviously something MagicJack wants to avoid and they can change the registration method as frequently as they want. On my side I may try to implement this unofficial authentication method (even if MagicJack will not be pleased with that ;) ).... but right now, I don't know how to implement it and wait for some feedback from MagicJack community hackers to find the non SIP compliant authentication method that match latest changes of magicjack.

So if you really want to have a sip client working with magic jack, you should cry to MagicJack directly. But as far as I understood, that's not their business model.

On my side no problem with adding some extra alternate authentication method. I just need to know what is this new obfuscated method ;)....

So as for this issue, sorry, but wontfix until either magicjack give me info about how to connect their server with their non standard authentication method or feedback from magicjack community on this method.

I try to remain independent of sip providers in order to have an application that works well for all providers ;).

However the wizard list can give you an idea of what are providers that are known to work well with CSipSimple. Some of these providers even have an official support of csipsimple and send me infos for the wizard in order to provide the best user experience.

You can also have a look to this wiki page : http://code.google.com/p/csipsimple/wiki/WhatIsSIP#SIP_providers It points some links that list sip providers.
